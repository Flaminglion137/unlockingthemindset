# You're already scrolling.

Not on your phone. Right now, in your head.

That's the thing this page is for.

---

## The mind doesn't wander by accident

It strays because staying put is *hard* and drifting is *easy* — and something, somewhere, built an entire economy on knowing that.

You didn't choose to open that app for the eleventh time today. Something noticed your attention flicker, and it was faster than you.

This page is slower. It's not trying to hook you. It's trying to catch you — for about ninety seconds — before you go back.

---

## Try this. Right now. Don't skip it.

Stop reading for five seconds. Actually stop.

Notice: what were you about to do the moment you finished this sentence?

That impulse — the one already reaching for the next tab, the next scroll, the next *anything* — that's the whole problem, captured live, in you, right now.

You just watched your own mind try to leave the room.

---

## Doomscrolling was never about the content

It's not that the content is good. Most of it, you won't remember in an hour.

It's that *stopping* takes more effort than *continuing*, and your brain will always take the path that costs less — even when it leads nowhere, even when it feels bad after, even when you *know* better while you're doing it.

Meaningless is easy. Easy is the entire trick.

---

## So what is this page, actually

A small interruption. A place with no feed, no infinite scroll, no next thing loading beneath your thumb.

Just an ending. On purpose.

Sit with it for a second before you go.

---

## Before you leave —

You made it to the bottom of a page with nothing else to give you. No autoplay. No "recommended next." No dopamine waiting at the bottom.

That's uncomfortable, isn't it?

**Good. That discomfort is the part of your mind that forgot it could just... stop.**

Go do one thing on purpose next. Anything. Just make it a choice, not a reflex.
